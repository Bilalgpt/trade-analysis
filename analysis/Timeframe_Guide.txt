TIMEFRAME GUIDE — SMART MONEY CONCEPTS
========================================
Which Timeframes to Use & How to Cross-Reference
========================================


========================================
THE GOLDEN RULE
========================================

Always read TOP → DOWN. Never bottom up.

High timeframe sets the direction.
Low timeframe gives the entry.

If you start on 5M and then check Daily — you are doing it backwards.
If Daily says bearish, no 1H setup can make you go long.


========================================
THE 3 LAYERS OF EVERY TRADE
========================================

Every trade has 3 jobs. Each job needs a different timeframe.

Layer 1 — BIAS    → What direction is the market going?
Layer 2 — ZONE    → Where will Smart Money react?
Layer 3 — ENTRY   → Exactly when do I get in?


========================================
BEST TIMEFRAME COMBINATIONS
========================================

SWING TRADER (hold 1–7 days) — Best for crypto
------------------------------------------------
Weekly  → Bias      (overall trend, major liquidity pools)
Daily   → Zone      (order blocks, FVGs, key levels)
4H      → Entry     (CHoCH, sweeps, confirmation)

Most reliable combination for crypto swing trading.

DAY TRADER (hold hours, same day)
----------------------------------
Daily   → Bias      (are we bullish or bearish today?)
4H      → Zone      (where are the OBs and FVGs?)
15M     → Entry     (CHoCH, sweep confirmation)
5M      → Precision (exact candle entry, tight stop)

========================================
HOW TO SET UP YOUR LAYOUT (3 Charts)
========================================

Open 3 charts side by side on TradingView:

LEFT chart    = Daily   (zoom out, big picture)
MIDDLE chart  = 4H      (your main analysis chart)
RIGHT chart   = 1H      (entry timing)


========================================
HOW TO CROSS-REFERENCE — STEP BY STEP
========================================

STEP 1 — START ON DAILY (Bias)
--------------------------------
Ask only ONE question: Is this market bullish or bearish?

Look for:
  - Is price above or below key swing highs/lows?
  - Where is the nearest BSL (buy side) and SSL (sell side)?
  - Overall structure: Higher highs = bullish. Lower lows = bearish.
  - Price above Kijun line = bullish. Below = bearish.

Write down: "Daily bias = BULLISH" or "BEARISH"

RULE:
  If Daily is bullish  → ONLY look for longs on lower timeframes
  If Daily is bearish  → ONLY look for shorts on lower timeframes
  Never trade against the Daily bias.


STEP 2 — GO TO 4H (Find the Zone)
------------------------------------
Ask: Where will price react?

Look for:
  - Equal lows / equal highs (liquidity pools)
  - Order blocks (last candle before a big move)
  - FVGs (imbalances from fast moves)
  - Is price in premium or discount zone?

Mark these zones with horizontal boxes.
These are your "areas of interest" — where you watch for entries.


STEP 3 — GO TO 1H (Wait for the Entry)
----------------------------------------
Ask: Is the setup actually happening right now?

Wait for:
  - Price reaches your 4H zone
  - A sweep occurs (wick through a liquidity level)
  - CHoCH forms on 1H
  - Price retests the CHoCH level / dips into FVG or OB
  - A clean bullish/bearish candle forms in the zone

THAT is your entry candle.

========================================

  → ENTER LONG HERE


========================================
THE ALIGNMENT RULE
========================================

The more timeframes agree → the stronger the trade.

Daily bullish + 4H zone hit + 1H CHoCH    = 3/3 = STRONG — take the trade
Daily bullish + 4H zone hit + no 1H CHoCH = 2/3 = WAIT for confirmation
Daily bearish + 4H setup bullish          = CONFLICT = SKIP entirely

Never trade when timeframes conflict.
If they disagree, there is no trade. Move on.


========================================
COMMON MISTAKES WITH TIMEFRAMES
========================================

MISTAKE 1: Starting on 1H or 15M without checking Daily first
  → You will take trades against the main trend and wonder why you lose
  → Always Daily first, no exceptions

MISTAKE 2: Using too many timeframes (5+ charts open)
  → Creates confusion, you will find signals everywhere
  → Stick to 3 timeframes maximum

MISTAKE 3: Switching timeframes after entering a trade
  → If you entered on 1H, manage it on 1H
  → Do not panic-switch to 5M and see scary candles
  → Trust the timeframe you planned on

MISTAKE 4: Ignoring Daily just because 4H looks perfect
  → A perfect 4H setup against the Daily trend loses most of the time
  → Daily always has authority

MISTAKE 5: Looking for entries on Daily
  → Daily is ONLY for bias, never for entries
  → Daily candles are too big, stops would be massive
  → Use Daily to read direction, 1H to enter

MISTAKE 6: Taking a trade when timeframes conflict
  → No confluence = no trade
  → Sitting on your hands IS a valid decision

========================================
SUMMARY IN ONE PARAGRAPH
========================================

Use 3 timeframes only: Daily for bias, 4H for zones, 1H for entry.
Always go top down — Daily first, then 4H, then 1H. Never reverse this.
If Daily says bullish, only take longs. Mark your OBs, FVGs, and
liquidity pools on 4H. Drop to 1H and wait for a sweep + CHoCH +
retest before entering. If all 3 timeframes agree, take the trade.
If any timeframe conflicts, skip it and wait for the next setup.
